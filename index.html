    Countries most exposed:
    🇩🇪 Germany🇬🇧 UK🇿🇦 S.Africa🇦🇺 Australia🇨🇴 Colombia🇺🇦 Ukraine

    The Strait closure is the largest single energy supply disruption in recorded history (IEA). Gulf producers have lost ~14mb/d of supply since February 28. All existing bypass pipelines cover less than 50% of the crude gap. For LNG there is no alternative route — Qatar's Ras Laffan is offline for an estimated 3–5 years.

    Refined products — the underassessed channel. IEA confirms diesel and jet fuel are more vulnerable to Hormuz disruption than crude. Gulf producers exported 3.3mb/d refined products in 2025 — now largely offline. European diesel hit $200/bbl. The indirect exposure chain: Gulf crude → Asian refinery → finished diesel → import-dependent economy. UK (imports 2.5× its diesel production), Germany (logistics and heavy industry), and Australia (~50% diesel indirectly exposed) all face this channel even without direct Gulf crude imports. This is a refinery grade configuration problem, not just a price problem — Asian refineries process Gulf medium-sour grades that US light-sweet WTI cannot replicate in diesel yield.

    Managed toll system — sourcing note. PSGA confirmed operational (US sanctioned it — independent corroboration). ~300+ ships transited after paying tolls; outbound: China 28%, other Asia 23%, India 19%, Europe 12%. Vessel counts are from PSGA/Iranian releases; AIS commercial data confirms increased but limited transits without independently verifying payment specifics. Toll volumes remain far below pre-war flows; IRGC retains instant revocation capability.

    Analysis Framework — How We Assess This

    This briefing applies a four-layer analytical framework:

    1. Structural constraints first. What physically cannot happen regardless of diplomacy? Hormuz mine-clearing takes months. Munitions replenishment takes years (Tomahawks to 2030). EU gas storage physics are fixed by October. These are not preferences — they are hard boundaries.
    2. Incentive asymmetry. Who has the shorter clock? Trump faces mid-July supply cliff + November midterms. The IRGC has no electoral clock, no bond market, no petrol constituency — and is monetising the stalemate through the toll system. Clock asymmetry structurally favours Iran.
    3. Four deal gaps — all must close simultaneously. Uranium, Lebanon/Hezbollah, Hormuz control, frozen funds. Any one of the four blocks Scenario 1. The Lebanon gap (Netanyahu deepening operations) is currently the least bridgeable.
    4. Scenario probability = base rate adjusted by active risk factors. Probabilities are research-informed analyst judgement, not actuarial modelling. The filter system shows directional sensitivity, not statistical precision. Treat them as ordinal rankings (Sc3 most likely, Sc2 second) rather than literal probabilities.

    The Freedman framing (Foreign Affairs, May 27): Trump cannot win on stated terms, cannot leave without appearing to lose, and cannot resume strikes given munitions depletion and War Powers constraints. This "exit problem" is the single most analytically useful frame for understanding why Scenario 2 (managed stalemate) and Scenario 3 (prolonged conflict) together account for ~79% of assessed probability.

    Methodology Note

    Scenario probabilities are the single canonical source — set once in the Scenarios tab, not repeated elsewhere. Risk filter toggles redistribute probability across scenarios; the resulting range reflects analytical sensitivity, not statistical precision. Likelihood × Impact scores are research-determined based on 100+ institutional sources. Country impact figures (CPI and GDP) are directional estimates calibrated against IMF, Oxford Economics, and Bloomberg Economics modelling ranges — they carry approximately ±1pp variance and are not point forecasts. Oil inventory data has known reporting variance across countries and companies; where multiple independent sources converge (IEA, EIA, Goldman Sachs, Bloomberg) confidence is higher but figures remain estimates. The analytical judgement connecting data to scenario changes is human, not automated.
