Situation Overview
The 2026 Iran War — Global Economic Shockwave
On 28 February 2026, the US and Israel launched Operation Epic Fury, killing Supreme Leader Khamenei. Iran closed the Strait of Hormuz — triggering the largest oil supply disruption in history (IEA). A fragile ceasefire began April 8 (Pakistan-mediated) but a dual blockade persists. Trump called Iran’s latest peace proposal “garbage” on May 11. The war has delivered an unintended strategic windfall to Russia and China — both exploiting US overextension without firing a shot. The 30-year US Treasury has breached 5% and the 10-year stands at 4.49% — the financial system is pricing structural risk, not just a temporary energy shock.
Today (May 12): Pentagon confirmed US military cost at $29 billion (up $4B in two weeks), excluding base repairs. Total economic cost est. $631B–$1T. The 30-year Treasury hit 5.03% and the 10-year reached 4.49% — 51 basis points from the 5% crisis threshold that triggers the bond vigilante scenario. These are not projections; they are live market signals that the war is creating a structural fiscal crisis alongside the energy shock.

Key Global Transmission Channels
Energy

Strait of Hormuz Closure

~27% of global seaborne oil and 20% of LNG transited Hormuz — a shock 2–3× the 1973 oil crisis. All existing bypass pipelines combined cover less than half the gap. LNG has no bypass whatsoever.

Food Security

Fertiliser & Planting Crisis

One-third of global fertiliser transits Hormuz. Farmers are reducing acreage and switching crops now. Harvest consequences arrive autumn 2026 and persist into 2027 regardless of when the war ends. See Food & Agriculture tab.

Financial Stress

Treasury Yield Crisis Emerging

30-year Treasury at 5.03%; 10-year at 4.49% as of today. $9.7 trillion in maturing US debt must be refinanced in 2026 at rates 150bp above the coupon. War prevents Fed cuts while increasing spending. The fiscal and energy crises are converging.

Ukraine Spillover

Russia & China Both Gain

Chatham House: “an economic gift for Putin.” China providing Iran satellite intelligence (Jilin-1) and dual-use technology. Urals crude above $70 vs. $59 budget. $100B+ Ukraine aid loan blocked. Western Patriot stocks diverted to Gulf.

Energy Exporters

Asymmetric Beneficiaries

US, Canada, and Australia recording elevated energy revenues. US crude exports near-record 12.9M bbl/day. Australia LNG spot +140%. Russia filling Hormuz supply gap to China and India at elevated prices.

Stagflation Trap

No Clean Exit for Central Banks

ECB postponed cuts March 19. Bank of England considering hikes. Fed holding at 3.5–3.75%. No central bank can cut (inflation) or hike (recession) cleanly. UK gilts market under notable stress. Corporate refinancing wall building.

Secondary Impact Analysis
Food Security & The Global Agricultural Planting Crisis
The food security impacts extend far beyond immediate price inflation. Fertiliser shortages and surging diesel costs are causing farmers to plant less, apply less fertiliser, and switch to lower-input crops right now. These decisions show up in harvest data months later — creating a deferred wave of food insecurity that persists long after any ceasefire. The food crisis will outlast the war regardless of when it ends.
Critical Timing Risk: Northern Hemisphere planting season is underway now. Southern Hemisphere planting opens later in 2026. The full agricultural impact is not yet visible and compounds into 2027 even if the war ends immediately. This is a deferred shock that market attention will miss until harvest shortfalls materialise.

The Planting Crisis — Cascade Mechanisms
Immediate — Now

Northern Hemisphere Planting Season

IFPRI identifies immediate impacts across USA, Canada, Europe, Ukraine, China, and India. USDA confirms: corn and wheat acreage down 3% each; soy up 4%. This is not a future risk — it is happening now, with harvest consequences arriving in autumn 2026.

Deferred — Coming Months

Southern Hemisphere Planting Window

Brazil, Argentina, South Africa, and Australia face the greatest deferred exposure. Brazil’s structural dependence — 85% of fertiliser imported, ~50% via Hormuz — makes it the single most exposed major agricultural producer globally.

Yield Cascade

2026–27 Harvest Cycle at Risk

IFPRI and World Bank: reduced fertiliser application does not show up immediately — it appears at harvest. Global grain stocks will decline materially in late 2026 and early 2027, pushing food prices higher precisely when the energy shock is expected to moderate.

Energy–Food Link

Diesel Costs Compound the Squeeze

Fuel for farm machinery is a major agricultural input. With diesel prices up sharply, total farm production costs have risen significantly independent of fertiliser. Smallholders in South Asia and Sub-Saharan Africa, who cannot access subsidies, face the hardest choices — directly driving WFP’s 45M hunger risk estimate.
