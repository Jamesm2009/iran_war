    Countries most exposed:
    🇩🇪 Germany🇬🇧 UK🇿🇦 S.Africa🇦🇺 Australia🇨🇴 Colombia🇺🇦 Ukraine

    The Strait closure is the largest single energy supply disruption in recorded history (IEA). Gulf producers have lost ~14mb/d since February 28. All existing bypass pipelines cover less than 50% of the crude gap. For LNG there is no alternative route — Qatar's Ras Laffan is offline with multi-year repair timelines confirmed by QatarEnergy.

    Refined products — the underassessed channel. IEA confirms diesel and jet fuel are more vulnerable to Hormuz disruption than crude. European diesel at $200/bbl. The indirect exposure chain: Gulf crude → Asian refinery → finished diesel → import-dependent economy. UK (imports 2.5× its diesel production), Germany (logistics and heavy industry), and Australia (~90% fuel import dependent) all face this channel even without direct Gulf crude imports. This is a refinery grade configuration problem — Asian refineries process Gulf medium-sour grades that US light-sweet WTI cannot replicate in diesel yield. Source: Valero & Labyrinth Consulting Services.

    Managed toll system — ISW Jun 7 update. An Iranian parliamentarian confirmed ships are paying average $1.5–2M per vessel in bartered goods and cryptocurrency. Iran's Environment Ministry is now formalising this as "maritime and environmental service fees." However: IRGC's Jun 7 ceasefire void declaration explicitly conditions the toll system on the Lebanon front — making it a conditional instrument, not a stable revenue stream. This destroys the stable equilibrium that Scenario 2 depended on.

    Methodology Note

    Scenario probabilities are the single canonical source — set once in the DATA object, propagated to the Probability Cone Panel. Risk filter toggles redistribute probability across scenarios; the resulting range reflects analytical sensitivity, not statistical precision. The Probability Cone width reflects what happens when all seven risk filters are simultaneously active vs inactive — analytical sensitivity range only. Country impact figures are directional estimates calibrated against IMF, Oxford Economics, and Bloomberg Economics modelling ranges — approximately ±1pp variance, not point forecasts. The analytical judgement connecting data to scenario changes is human, not automated.
