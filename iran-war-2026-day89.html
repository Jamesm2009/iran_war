↑ Updated May 28

A Pakistan-mediated ceasefire took effect April 8, but a dual blockade persists and the ceasefire has been repeatedly breached by both sides. The IRGC has no electoral clock, no bond market constituency, and no gasoline-price voters — and is rebuilding missile silos faster than negotiations are progressing. As of this morning, the IRGC has escalated geographically, striking a US base in Kuwait.

IRGC Strikes US Airbase in Kuwait

Iran's IRGC claimed a strike on a US airbase — identified by Kuwait's air defense activation as Ali Al Salem Air Base — at 4:50am local time (0120 GMT). This is the first confirmed IRGC strike on a Gulf state US installation during the ceasefire period. The IRGC framed it as a "serious warning" and stated: "If repeated, our response will be more decisive." No immediate US military response has been confirmed. This represents a deliberate geographic widening of the conflict — from the Strait to Kuwait is not an accident.

What Today Changes
⚠
The Kuwait strike is analytically significant for three reasons: (1) It proves the IRGC is willing to strike Gulf state soil — not just the Strait — during a nominal ceasefire. (2) It tests US commitment to Gulf partner defense, which affects Saudi and UAE reliability for any deal framework. (3) It narrows Trump's options: responding escalates; not responding signals weakness and emboldens the IRGC. The IRGC chose this action the morning after Camp David — that timing is deliberate.

Deferred — Autumn 2026

Energy–Food Link
Fuel for farm machinery is a major agricultural input. With diesel prices up sharply, total farm production costs have risen significantly independent of fertiliser. Smallholders in South Asia and Sub-Saharan Africa face the hardest choices.
